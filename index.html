Story

Paolo Cesare Maldini (born 26 June 1968) is an Italian former footballer who played as a left back or central defender for A.C. Milan and Italy national team. He spent all 25 seasons of his career at Serie A club A.C. Milan, before retiring at the age of 41 in 2009.

Trophies

He won 26 trophies with Milan: the Champions League five times, seven Serie A titles, one Coppa Italia, five Supercoppa Italiana, five European Super Cups, two Intercontinental Cups, and one FIFA Club World Cup.

Present

In May 2015, it was announced that Maldini became the co-owner of NASL club Miami FC, alongside television rights entrepreneur Riccardo Silva, in hope that the team would be able to join the MLS in future.
